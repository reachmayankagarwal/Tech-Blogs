<div className="oc-grid">

<div className="oc-card oc-c" data-num="01">
  <h3>Problem</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Card-not-present (CNP) fraud reached <b>$816M</b> in CY2024 — <b>90%</b> of all Australian card fraud and rising 19% YoY.</li>
    <li>In verbal MOTO the full card is <b>exposed to the agent</b>; paper order forms leave card data unsafe if lost or copied.</li>
    <li>Verbal MOTO sits in <b>SAQ D scope</b> (over 300 controls); penalties for non-compliance run <b>$5k–$100k/month</b>.</li>
    <li>The <b>Scams Prevention Framework</b> imposes new sector obligations from <b>1 Jul 2026</b> (Tier 1 penalty up to $50M).</li>
  </ul>
  <p className="oc-note">MOTO is a primary contributor to CNP fraud — not the source of the full $913M headline.</p>
</div>

<div className="oc-card oc-b" data-num="02">
  <h3>Users &amp; Customers</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Merchants</b> taking phone &amp; mail payments: utilities, insurance, councils, healthcare, professional services.</li>
    <li><b>Contact-centre agents</b> currently handling raw card numbers.</li>
    <li><b>Less-digital customers</b> who rely on phone &amp; paper and are excluded by payment links.</li>
    <li><b>The adopting bank</b> as scheme participant, app owner and merchant-services provider.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div className="oc-card oc-g" data-num="03">
  <h3>Solution</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>Bank PayTo for MOTO:</b> a PayID on the form or over the phone replaces card data entirely.</li>
    <li>Customer approves a merchant-initiated pull payment <b>in their own bank's app</b> — async, real-time, under 60s.</li>
    <li><b>Zero card data</b> held — collapses scope to SAQ A (22 controls).</li>
    <li>Secures <b>both channels</b>: paper forms stay accessible, telephone orders need no card capture.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

  <h3>Key Risks &amp; Open Questions</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><b>NPP irreversibility</b> — no chargeback; needs a MOTO-specific dispute design.</li>
    <li><b>APP / social-engineering scam risk</b> — mitigate via ABN-verified merchants, verified name in push, velocity limits, first-payment cool-down.</li>
    <li><b>Credit-card support</b> — PayTo debits bank accounts only; cards need a Phase 2 tokenisation bridge.</li>
    <li><b>ePayments Code &amp; PayID enumeration</b> — regulatory coverage and privacy questions to clear in discovery.</li>
    <li><b>No MOTO PayTo API spec or vendor accreditation framework</b> — must be designed with AP+.</li>
